Step 3. Selecting files to download

After Step 2 has completed. All available downloads (excluding files that are already on your system) are displayed in the Downloads form (pictured below in Figure 1).

Figure 1. Downloads selection form

Selecting

To select the files that you would like to download from the OU website: check or uncheck the checkboxes on the left side of the table to include or exclude respectively.

To check or uncheck all items in the list: click on the Check all or Uncheck all buttons respectively. (These are located in the bottom-left of the window).

Proceeding or cancelling

When you have finished making your selections, click on the Download... button and you will proceed to Step 3.

Alternatively, if you would like to cancel: click on the Cancel button. This will abandon the download process (your user profile will be signed-out) and you will have to start again from Step 1.

Grouping and sorting

The files are grouped by OU course module code and the groupings are in order of enrollment. Presently, this cannot be changed.

By default, the files are sorted in alphabetical order based on Name. To sort the files based on Type, Size (using numerical order), or Location: click on the respective table heading.

To alternate between ascending and descending order: click again on the already selected table heading. This will toggle from ascending to descending or from descending to ascending based on the current state.

Tallying

The number of files selected (along with their total combined size) are displayed in the bottom-left corner of the window. These numbers are updated automatically as files are selected or deselected.

Names, types, sizes, and locations

Note: this information is gathered directly from the OU website with no pre-processing or analysis being performed. File sizes are not exact and file types reflect OU terminology (notice: "PDF version" and "PDF version of web pages" above in Figure 1).

Characters within the Name and Location fields that are not permitted in file or directory names such as ':' are normalised prior to saving.
